Mutton Kosha

Mutton Kosha is a flavorful Bengali dish featuring tender mutton slow-cooked in a rich blend of spices. This aromatic curry, known for its depth of flavor, combines succulent meat with a medley of spices, creating a dish that's both hearty and satisfying. Perfect for special occasions or a comforting family meal, Mutton Kosha pairs beautifully with steamed rice or naan bread, offering a taste of traditional Bengali cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil (or any cooking oil)
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 2-3 whole green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust according to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as needed

Steps

  1. Marinate the mutton pieces with yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker. Add the whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, cumin seeds) and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Cook for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Now, add the marinated mutton pieces and cook on high heat until they are seared and browned on all sides.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the mutton cook in its juices for about 45 minutes to 1 hour. If using a pressure cooker, cook for about 4-5 whistles.
  8. Once the mutton is tender and cooked through, adjust the seasoning and consistency of the curry. Add water if needed to achieve the desired thickness.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan bread.
